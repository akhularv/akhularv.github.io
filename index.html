Experimental Neurobiology

Multi-Electrode Array Organotypic Slice Recordings

At the Salk Institute Computational Neurobiology Lab, I designed this project to investigate the real electrophysiological and morphological changes that happen in thalamocortical circuits after maternal immune activation treatment. I used RNA seq analysis and paper based research to figure out the ideal antibodies for labeling these circuits, the ideal slice angle to preserve them, and the procedure needed to keep developing mouse brain tissue alive in vitro. I also designed the broader data collection strategy and am now building toward machine learning based analysis as the project enters its first data collection phase.

  • Thalamocortical circuits
  • Organotypic culture
  • Antibody strategy
  • ML ready analysis

The jist: Building the full system needed to capture circuit level change, not just measure pieces of it

Neural Decoding System

Perceptogram: Bayesian VAE Upgrade

In collaboration with Simon Fei from the De Sa Lab, I took an EEG decoding framework that originally relied on standard latent regression and was largely specific to individual subjects, then pushed it toward a more generalizable cross subject approach. The system combines variational autoencoders with Bayesian learning, which better matches the biological variability and unpredictability of EEG signals rather than forcing the model into a cleaner signal than reality gives us. Right now, I am doing post hoc work to understand how the prior weight matrix is shaped by the first eight participants, and how adding a ninth participant changes the model's internal structure through weight shifts and perturbation analysis.

  • Probabilistic decoding
  • Variational autoencoders
  • Hierarchical Bayes
  • Cross subject generalization

The jist: Trying to make neural decoding generalize without pretending biology is cleaner than it is

Machine Learning System

Cross Species Treatment Effect VAE

This project at the Salk Institute aims to understand the mechanistic biology behind maternal immune activation by analyzing RNA seq data through a VAE based network. The model encodes the signal across separate latent spaces for treatment, biological structure, and batch effects, then learns to reconstruct the original data through the decoder. By perturbing the latent nodes and linking them back to gene ontology and broader biological concepts, we can start to see which systems the treatment is actually changing, whether that is neurodevelopment, immune response, or other major biological programs.

  • Transcriptomic modeling
  • Interpretable latent space
  • Treatment disentanglement
  • Biological mechanism

The jist: Using latent space to move from raw expression shifts to actual biological mechanism

Scientific Interface Tool

3D Brain Slice Visualizer

This tool pulls meshes from the Allen Brain Atlas to visualize the exact slicing pattern needed to capture the thalamocortical circuitry we are targeting. The user can rotate the brain, toggle different regions, move the slicer through any angle or direction, and then view the resulting slice in both 3D and 2D. The point is to make slice planning much more precise, so we can stop guessing whether a given angle will actually preserve the structures we need.

  • Atlas based tooling
  • Oblique slice planning
  • Spatial neuroscience
  • Interactive planning

The jist: Turning slice design into something you can actually see, test, and refine before cutting

Brain Computer Interface

VR Puzzle Game with Adaptive EEG Support

At SCCN, I am working on a VR puzzle based BCI project where we collect EEG and eye tracking data from subjects as they play the game. When the game becomes too difficult, an AI support agent appears and adjusts its help based on the player's brain state in real time. We use EEGLAB during live collection, and the broader goal is to push toward larger and more universal EEG models that can better dissect cognitive state and support richer BCI interaction over time.

  • Brain state decoding
  • VR interaction
  • EEGLAB in real time
  • Adaptive assistance

The jist: Building toward BCI systems that respond when the brain starts to struggle, not after the user already fails

Future

My Future

01

Circuit-Resolution Neural Signal Understanding

I've done work with EEG and MEA signals, which tend to capture geographic event potentials more effectively than circuit-level activity. If given the right hardware, I would like to turn precise electrical signals into neurobiological activity to understand what is happening on an electrocellular basis.

02

Concepts of Cognition, Learning and Neurobiology Integrated into ML Systems

The most efficient ML systems are based around brain architecture that was formed by millions of years of evolution. There is much to be learned from applying computational neurobiology paradigms to machine learning.

03

Reverse the Known Processes - Learn How to Stimulate the Brain to Encode Information

Raw stimulation runs into issues like plasticity and intracellular shifts. BCI scientists will have to learn how to safely stimulate the brain with stimulation that is actually encoded with meaning to that brain. Optogenetic and intracellular manipulation are tactics I can see being used.
